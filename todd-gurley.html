Todd Gurley | Early Life, Family & Education  

N.F.L. athlete Todd Gurley was born on August 3, 1994, in Baltimore, Maryland, to Todd Gerome Gurley and Darlene Simmons. 

Moreover, Gurley has four siblings: Princeton, Davon, Shannon, and Tarik. 

Apart from this, not much data is available regarding Gurley’s family. 

Age, Height, and Body Measurements

The rising athlete Todd turned 29 Years Old in 2023. Gurley possesses dark brown eyes and black-colored hair. 

He has an athletic body frame, is blessed with an impressive height of 1.85 m, and weighs 224 lbs. 

Education 

Todd attended Tarboro High School in Tarboro, North Carolina. During his school years, Gurley was a three-sport star in basketball, soccer, and track.

Moreover, he played in the position of running back and defensive back for his school team.

Likewise, during Gurley’s junior season, he recorded 1,472 rushing yards and 26 touchdowns, 79 tackles, a forced fumble, and an interception.

Because of his performance, Todd won Rocky Mount Telegram All-Area Offensive Player of the Year during the 2010 season.

Similarly, as a senior in high school, Todd became the North Carolina Associated Press Player of the Year with 38 touchdowns and 2,600 yards.

Furthermore, Todd helped his school football team conquer the 2A North Carolina state championship as a senior.

Gurley recorded 242 yards rush and four touchdowns during the championship while playing against Lincolnton High School.

Todd was also a world-class sprinter and hurdler for the high school’s track & field team.

He participated in Team U.S.A. in the 110-meters hurdles during the 2011 World Youth Championships.

He won the 3rd position in the prelims while recording a career-best time of 13.66 seconds and finished fifteenth overall.

According to Rivals.com, Todd was considered a four-star recruit and was registered as the fifth-best running back in his year.

After graduating from high school, Todd joined the University of Georgia. 

Todd Gurley | Career and Profession 

Before jumping into Gurley’s professional career, let’s look into his college career first. 

College Career 

Freshman Season

Todd began his freshman season while representing Georgia as a backup for redshirt sophomore player Ken Malcome.

During his first game against the Buffalo Bulls, Gurley recorded 100 yards on eight carries, including two rushing touchdowns.

Additionally, Todd took over as the starter during the second game of the season against S.E.C. East opponent Missouri with 65 yards rushing at ten carries during a 41–20 victory. 

During the game against Georgia Southern, Todd became the second true freshman in Georgia’s history to record 1,000 yards rush, and the only other player was Herschel Walker in 1980.

Similarly, during the regular-season finale against Georgia Tech, Todd recorded 12 carries for 97 rushing yards with two rushing touchdowns in the 42–10 victory.

As a result, Georgia won the face-off against Alabama in the S.E.C. Championship and S.E.C. East. 

Furthermore, during the 2012 season, Todd started in 12 out of 14 games and recorded 1,385 rushing yards on 222 carries, including 17 rushing touchdowns.

Gurley’s 17 rushing touchdowns became the tied record for third in a single season in the school’s history.

Likewise, Todd finished second in the S.E.C. in rushing yards, and the Associated Press named him the First-Team All-SEC.

Gurley was one of two true freshman running backs to achieve such honor in 2012, and the other player was T. J. Yeldon of Alabama.

Sophomore Season

On August 31, 2013, during Georgia’s season opener held at Clemson’s Memorial Stadium, Tod led the team with a career-high 154 rushing yards at 12 carries and two rushing touchdowns.

Although the Bulldogs failed to win the game with a score of 38–35, Todd had an impressive performance with a 75-yard touchdown run early during the first quarter.

After his sophomore season, the Associated Press named Todd the Second-Team All-SEC.

Moreover, in the spring of 2013, Todd joined the Georgia Bulldogs indoor track squad. Gurley competed in the 60-meter hurdles and registered the seventh-fastest time in school history with 8.12 seconds, taking sixth place at the V.T. Elite Meet. 

Junior Season

Furthermore, during the season opener against Clemson on August 30, 2014, Todd rushed for 198 yards, and three touchdowns with 15 carry to go, including a 100-yard kickoff return touchdown in the 45–21 victory.

Likewise, on September 27, against Tennessee, he had 28 carries for 208 rushing yards and two rushing touchdowns in the 35–32 win.

Gurley’s 208 rushing yards record was the most made in a single game by a player in Georgia’s history since Garrison Hearst in 1992. 

Suspension 

On October 9, 2014, the University of Georgia indefinitely suspended Todd due to an alleged violation of N.C.A.A. rules.

The investigation took two days to ascertain that Todd received $3,000 over two years for his signed memorabilia and autographs.

Thus, Gurley missed the next two games before the NCAA confirmed its decision to suspend Todd for four total games.

Likewise, Todd sat out for two more games and became eligible to play during the annual rivalry game.

During his first game back from suspension on November 15, 2014, Todd tore his A.C.L. and ended his collegiate career at the University of Georgia.

In six games, Gurley finished the 2014 season with nine rushing touchdowns and 123 carries for 911 rushing yards.

Moreover, Todd decided to leave his senior season and enrolled in the 2015 NFL Draft.

His 3,285 rushing yards records were second overall and his 36 rushing touchdowns tied in the second position in the school’s history at his departure.

Professional Career

St. Louis / Los Angeles Rams

Matt was selected as the 10th overall pick by the St. Louis Rams in the 2015 N.F.L. Draft during the first round. Todd was the highest-drafted running back out of the University of Georgia since Tim Worley in 1989.

Todd’s rehabilitation from the injury sustained during his last collegiate game went ahead of schedule.

During the team’s preseason, he did not play and practiced without wearing pads. Shortly after, Todd was medically cleared for play by St. Louis medical team. 

Thus, on September 27, 2015, Todd made his NFL debut against the Pittsburgh Steelers during Week 3 of the 2015 season.

He eased into action and finished the game with six rushes for nine yards.

Successful Rookie Season

Todd scored his first N.F.L. touchdown on October 25, 2015, while playing against the Browns in the Week 7 matchup.

Due to his 566 yards in the first four N.F.L. starts, Todd became the most prolific rusher in his first four N.F.L. tournaments. 

Moreover, during week 8, Todd’s jersey and cleats became part of the Pro Football Hall of Fame.

Likewise, in Week 15, Todd scored a touchdown, rushed for 48 yards, and became the third rookie in the Rams’ history to rush for 1,000 yards during the season. 

Similarly, in Week 16, Todd rushed for 85 yards on 19 while playing against the Seattle Seahawks, and became the second Rams rookie, to rush for ten touchdowns and 1,000 yards. 

Furthermore, on December 22, 2015, Todd became part of the 2016 Pro Bowl and 2015 NFL All-Rookie Team. 

Todd was one of the five rookies selected for the Pro Bowl. He also won the N.F.L. Offensive Rookie of the Year honors and became the 22nd player on the N.F.L. Top 100 Players of 2016.

After Change in Head Coach 

Following the firing of head coach Jeff Fisher, Sean McVay became the Rams’ new head coach. 

Todd started the 2017 season while executing a reliable performance against the Indianapolis Colts, where he recorded 19 carries for 40 rushing yards with a rushing touchdown. 

His breakout game came in Week 3 while playing against the San Francisco 49ers, where he ran for 113 yards and two touchdowns while catching five passes for 36 yards and one touchdown.

He won the N.F.C. Offensive Player of the Month honors for September after recording 140 receiving yards, six total touchdowns, and 241 rushing yards.

Likewise, his streak continued during week four games as well. 

Moreover, Todd led the N.F.L. in yards-from-scrimmage and total touchdowns and won the N.F.C. Offensive Player of the Week honors once again. 

N.F.C. Offensive Player

Furthermore, during Week 15, Todd had a career-best four touchdowns and became the first player of Ram to score four touchdowns in a single game. 

His performance during Week 15 earned him an honor from N.F.C. Offensive Player of the Week. On December 19, 2017, Todd became part of the second Pro Bowl as a starter.

Similarly, in Week 16, Todd caught ten passes for 158 yards, including two receiving touchdowns to go along with 118 rushing yards, and won the N.F.C. Offensive Player of the Week honors for the second straight week.

Likewise, Todd won the NFC Offensive Player of the Month for the second time that season. In addition, he ended the season leading the league in scoring with 114 points.

Also, Todd was the 16th player in N.F.L. history with 750 rushing and receiving yards and the eighth player to record a 1,300 and 750 score.

Moreover, Todd played his career-first postseason game during the Wild Card Round against the Atlanta Falcons on January 6, 2018. 

After a stellar 2017 season, Todd won the N.F.L. Offensive Player of the Year and was ranked sixth by his fellow players on the N.F.L. Top 100 Players of 2018.

Contract Extension with the Rams 

On July 24, 2018, Gurley signed a four-year contract extension worth $60 million and $45 million guaranteed with the Rams, which made him the highest-paid running back in the N.F.L.

Todd scored the Rams’ first touchdown of the season, going 19 yards to the end zone while playing against the Oakland Raiders during Week 1 on Monday Night Football. 

Furthermore, in Week 6, Todd ran 28 times for a career-high 208 yards, which earned him NFC Offensive Player of the Week.

Likewise, Todd won the N.F.C. Offensive Player of the Month for October after recording 462 yards rush and seven touchdowns. 

Continuous Successful Honors

During week 10, Todd set a new team record by scoring a touchdown in 13 straight games and broke the record set by Elroy Hirsch.

Moreover, during week 12, Gurley won the N.F.C. Offensive Player of the Week for the second time in 2018.

He also went for his third Pro Bowl and first-team All-Pro in his four professional seasons.

During the Super Bowl, Todd’s injury status was heavily discussed, with reports claiming that he was healthy, while other words stated that his knee was significantly more hurt. 

On March 2, 2019, the medical reports asserted that Todd had arthritis in his left knee.

Final Season with the Rams 

Furthermore, during the season’s midpoint, Todd tied in the 29th position in the N.F.L. in rushing yards, in contradiction to 2018 when he was the leader and during the 2017 season when he was in the fourth position. 

Overall, Todd ended the 2019 season with 857 rushing yards and 12 rushing touchdowns, 31 receptions for 207 receiving yards, and two receiving touchdowns.

Moreover, on March 19, 2020, the Los Angeles Rams released Todd from the team.

Atlanta Falcons

On April 6, 2020, Todd signed a one-year contract worth $5.5 million with the Atlanta Falcons, including a $2 million signing bonus and $500,000 incentives if, in the future, he records 13 touchdowns or 1,000 rushing yards.

Moreover, Todd made his debut with the Falcons during Week 1 while playing against the Seattle Seahawks. During the game, he rushed 14 times for 56 yards. 

Overall, Todd ended the 2020 season with nine rushing touchdowns with, 25 receptions for 164 receiving yards and 195 carries for 678 rushing yards.
